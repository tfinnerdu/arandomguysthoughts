I bought this domain two years ago.

Two years. I set up the YouTube channel. I thought about what I'd write, what I'd say, how I'd say it. And then I did absolutely nothing with any of it.

If you're a guy reading this, you probably get that. We're great at thinking about things. Planning things. Building the perfect version of something in our heads. And then never actually doing it because it's not ready yet, or the timing isn't right, or — if we're being honest — because putting yourself out there is terrifying.

So here I am. Doing it anyway.

## The Short Version

I'm Todd. I'm a developer, a dad, a gamer, a cook, and a guy who's been through some stuff. I live in Nebraska. I smoke brisket. I play D&D with a group of dads. I coach esports. I watch too much sports. I'm deeply, almost annoyingly, average — and I think that's exactly why this blog needs to exist.

Because "average" guys don't talk about the hard things. We don't talk about grief, about depression, about the moments where you're sitting in your car in the driveway for an extra five minutes because you need to get your head right before you walk inside. We don't talk about how communication in relationships is something you have to actually learn and practice. We don't talk about the assumptions we make — about ourselves, about the people around us — that quietly wreck things if we let them.

## The Longer Version

I had a younger brother. He died by suicide.

I'm not going to make this whole blog about that, because grief isn't a brand and my brother's death isn't content. But it would be dishonest to not name it as the thing that cracked something open in me. It changed how I think about a lot of things — masculinity, vulnerability, what it means to "be strong," and how much damage that phrase can do when it's the only tool in your emotional toolbox.

After he died, I realized how many guys I knew were carrying heavy things and telling no one. Not because they didn't want to talk, but because they'd been taught — explicitly or implicitly — that talking about it makes you weak. That feelings are something you manage, not something you share. That the right response to pain is to tough it out.

That's wrong. And I think most of us know it. We just don't hear it from enough people who look and sound like us.

## So What Is This?

This blog is going to be a lot of things. That's the point. Life isn't one thing.

Some days I'll write about mental health and the stuff men don't talk about. Other days I'll post a recipe for smoked ribs. Sometimes I'll rant about a game I'm playing or a sports take I can't keep to myself. There will be nerdy stuff. There will be random stuff. There will probably be posts that are too long and posts that are just a few sentences.

It's called "A Random Guy's Thoughts" because that's genuinely what it is. No expertise being claimed. No brand being built. Just a person thinking out loud and hoping some of it lands for someone.

## Why Now?

Because two years of "I'll get to it" is enough. Because waiting for the perfect moment is just a polished version of staying quiet. And because if there's one thing losing my brother taught me, it's that you don't always get more time to say the things you want to say.

So I'm saying them.

Welcome. Stick around if you want. It's going to be a weird ride.

*— Todd*
